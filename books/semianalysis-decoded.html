Inside the AI Machine

A plain-language guide to how the AI industry actually works — the chips, the economics, the strategy — for product managers new to the AI hardware world. Each issue takes one dense, important development in AI infrastructure and decodes it, with three things on every page: (1) plain language, no assumed knowledge of semiconductors; (2) the token economics behind why a decision matters; (3) the implication for the AI features you build, the contracts you sign, and the roadmaps you set. Five Foundations primers build the vocabulary from zero — and inside any article, hover an underlined term for an instant definition.

About this book

 

The deep mechanics of AI infrastructure are dense, technical, and usually written for chip engineers and traders — not for product managers. This book takes one important development at a time, keeps the analysis honest, drops the jargon, and builds the vocabulary from the ground up. Read the five Foundations primers first, then the issues in any order. Hover any underlined term for an instant definition.
