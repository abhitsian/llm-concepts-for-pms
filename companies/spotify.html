Gustav Soderstrom, Co-CEO, Q4 2025

"The next evolution of Spotify [is] delivering the world's most intelligent, agentic media platform, one that you can literally talk to, that fully understands each individual listener and puts them in the driver's seat. It's about moving from a passive experience to an interactive one."

Gustav Soderstrom, Co-CEO, Q4 2025

The AI Strategy

Spotify is building the most defensible AI data moat in media: the taste graph. This dataset maps language to subjective preferences (music, podcasts, audiobooks) across hundreds of millions of users. As Soderstrom says, “you can’t just have an LLM commoditize it as a fact, the way you can commoditize Wikipedia.” Taste is inherently personal and requires massive-scale behavioral data to model.

The AI DJ (90M subscribers, 4B+ hours of listening) proves the concept: an AI product that leverages proprietary taste data to create an experience no competitor can replicate. Prompted Playlist extends this to on-demand creation. The vision — an “intelligent, agentic media service you can literally talk to” — is the end state.

The engineering transformation is equally significant. Top developers “have not written a single line of code since December” using an internal AI coding system built on Claude Code. Managing code from mobile devices isn’t a productivity hack — it’s a fundamental change in how software gets built, from a company that practices what it preaches about AI.

The Counterintuitive Insight

Spotify uses AI to commoditize the thing it pays for (music) while monetizing the thing it creates for free (discovery). Every song is available on every streaming platform. The moat isn’t the catalog — it’s the taste graph that knows what you want to hear before you do. AI makes Spotify’s real product (recommendations) more powerful while making the commodity it licenses (music) less differentiating. The more AI advances, the wider the gap between “has every song” and “knows which song you need right now.”
