Yelp on AI

Transforming from a discovery platform to an "answers and actions" framework, using AI to connect consumers with local businesses and building AI SaaS tools for those businesses.

What the CEO Said

"We meaningfully increased our focus on transforming Yelp with AI, believing that combining authentic human-generated content with advanced AI presents a significant opportunity to redefine how people connect with local businesses."

Jeremy Stoppelman, CEO, Q4 2025

"[We are] confident in our ability to transform Yelp with AI in ways that play to the strengths of our business, and [we] plan to increase investments in 2026, inclusive of the recent acquisition of Hatch, to capitalize on this opportunity and deliver long-term sustainable growth."

Jeremy Stoppelman, CEO, Q4 2025

"[Yelp Host is] fantastic and we are seeing a great response from restaurants with sales above initial expectations. On the Receptionist side, we got the opportunity to pair up with Hatch, which accelerates our roadmap there by a couple years."

Jeremy Stoppelman, CEO, Q4 2025

The Numbers

  • Yelp Assistant Request-A-Quote project submissions increased more than 400% year-over-year in 2025
  • Request-A-Quote projects increased approximately 15% YoY (excluding paid search projects)
  • Yelp Host has answered more than 190,000 calls and handled thousands of reservations since launch
  • Data licensing revenue grew 17% YoY, accelerating to 33% in Q4 (driven by OpenAI partnership)
  • 2025 net revenue: $1.46 billion, up 4% YoY; net income: $146 million, up 10% YoY (10% margin)

AI Products & Initiatives

  • Yelp Assistant — AI-powered conversational tool for consumers, shifting from discovery to "answers and actions" across categories; 400% increase in project submissions
  • Yelp Host — AI-powered call answering service for restaurants, handles reservations and soon phone food orders
  • Yelp Receptionist (via Hatch acquisition) — AI-powered lead management SaaS for businesses, automates converting leads into customers
  • OpenAI Partnership — Licensing Yelp's high-quality local content for local intent queries in AI platforms
  • Strategic Pivot — Moving from pure discovery platform to AI-powered actions and SaaS for local businesses

The AI Strategy

Yelp is executing a strategic pivot from discovery platform to AI-powered actions and B2B SaaS. Yelp Assistant (400% growth in project submissions) shifts the product from “read reviews” to “get things done.” Yelp Host and Yelp Receptionist (via Hatch acquisition) sell AI tools directly to the businesses Yelp lists — a B2B revenue stream that didn’t exist two years ago.

The data licensing play (17% growth, accelerating to 33% in Q4) turns Yelp’s content from a traffic driver into a data product. OpenAI needs Yelp’s review data to answer local questions accurately. This creates a revenue stream that benefits from AI disruption rather than being threatened by it.

The “answers and actions” framework is the right strategic response to AI search. If Google AI Overviews can answer “best pizza near me,” Yelp can’t compete on discovery alone. But if Yelp Assistant can actually call the restaurant, make a reservation, and order food, it’s doing something AI search can’t.

The Counterintuitive Insight

Yelp is selling the ammunition to the army invading its territory — and getting richer from it. Google AI Overviews and ChatGPT can answer “best pizza near me” without Yelp. But those AI systems need Yelp’s review data to answer accurately. Data licensing revenue grew 33% in Q4, driven by the OpenAI partnership. The more AI search advances, the more valuable Yelp’s corpus of local business reviews becomes for training and grounding those AI systems.
