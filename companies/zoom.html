Zoom on AI

Evolving from a video conferencing tool into an "AI-first system of action" -- bundling AI Companion for free to drive adoption, then monetizing with Custom AI Companion for enterprise workflow automation.

What the CEO Said

"We're evolving Zoom into an AI-first system of action."

Eric Yuan, CEO (Q4 FY2025, February 2025)

"AI, AI, and AI."

Eric Yuan, CEO, when asked about investment priorities (Q4 FY2025, February 2025)

"Each of our top 10 deals in the quarter included paid AI; 7 represented competitive displacements of other CCaaS vendors."

Eric Yuan, CEO (Q4 FY2025, February 2025)

The Numbers

  • AI Companion monthly active users grew 68% quarter-over-quarter in Q4 FY2025
  • AI Companion monthly active users more than tripled year-over-year; side panel users more than doubled QoQ
  • Custom AI Companion priced at ~$12/user/month (launched April 2025)
  • Q4 FY2025 revenue: $1.247B, up 5.3% YoY; full year $4.869B, up 4.4% YoY
  • CEO Eric Yuan used his own AI avatar to deliver opening comments on earnings calls

AI Products & Initiatives

  • AI Companion -- free AI assistant for meeting summaries, chat, email, and docs, included at no cost for all paid tiers
  • Custom AI Companion -- paid add-on with custom agents, workflow builders, third-party integrations, and enterprise knowledge retrieval
  • AI-powered Contact Center (CCaaS) with AI-driven customer service
  • AI avatars for asynchronous video communication
  • Yuan's vision: AI will eventually shorten the workweek

The AI Strategy

Zoom is executing a “land free, expand paid” AI strategy. AI Companion (free for all paid tiers) drives adoption and retention, while Custom AI Companion ($12/user/month) captures revenue from enterprises that need custom agents and workflow automation. This mirrors the freemium playbook but applied to AI features.

Yuan’s “AI-first system of action” repositioning reflects the existential challenge: in a world where “just a video call” is commoditized, Zoom needs AI to justify its existence. The CCaaS (Contact Center as a Service) expansion — where 7 of the top 10 deals displaced competing vendors — shows AI driving competitive wins in adjacent markets.

The AI avatar on the earnings call is both a product demo and a philosophical statement. If the CEO can be replaced by an AI avatar for routine communications, what does that say about the future of meetings themselves? Zoom is the one company in this list honestly wrestling with the possibility that AI might obsolete its core product category.

The Counterintuitive Insight

Zoom gives away AI to survive — and it might be genius. AI Companion is free for all paid tiers, an unusual move for a company fighting commoditization. The logic: if AI features are free, they become table stakes. If they become table stakes, they drive retention. If they drive retention, Zoom keeps its base while competitors charge for similar features. In a market where “just a video call” is worthless, free AI isn’t generosity — it’s the new moat.
