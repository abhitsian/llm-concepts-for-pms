The AI Strategy

Meta is executing commoditize-the-complement at a scale never attempted before. Open-sourcing Llama — built with $115-135B in annual capex — is a deliberate strategy to ensure the model layer is a commodity. If models are free, value accrues to the application + distribution layer. Meta has 3.35 billion daily active users across its apps. No competitor comes close.

The strategic logic: spend whatever it takes to make the model layer free, because Meta monetizes the application layer (ads across Facebook, Instagram, WhatsApp, Messenger). Every company using Llama reinforces Meta’s position without competing with Meta’s actual business. Spotify, AT&T, and DoorDash using Llama in production is the proof.

Meta AI reaching 1 billion monthly active users (the fastest AI platform growth in history) validates the distribution thesis. When you have 3.3B daily users and integrate AI across every touchpoint, adoption is gravity. The question isn’t whether Meta AI is the best model — it’s whether it’s the most used. Usage creates data, data improves the model, and the flywheel accelerates.

The Counterintuitive Insight

Meta spends more on AI infrastructure than most companies earn in total revenue — and gives the results away for free. The $115-135B 2026 capex isn’t charity; it’s the most expensive competitive moat ever built. By making Llama free, Meta ensures no competitor can charge for the model layer. When the model is free, the only thing that matters is distribution. And nobody out-distributes a company with 3.35 billion daily active users.
