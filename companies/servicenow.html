The AI Strategy

ServiceNow is positioning as “The Great Consolidator” — the platform that replaces hundreds of point SaaS solutions with AI-powered workflows. McDermott’s “we don’t live in the SaaS neighborhood” signals a fundamental repositioning from software-as-a-service to agents-as-a-service.

The AI Control Tower concept — governing 2.2 billion AI agents by 2030 — is a bet that orchestration and governance will be more valuable than any individual SaaS application. When every enterprise has thousands of AI agents, someone needs to manage, monitor, and secure them. ServiceNow wants to be that layer.

Now Assist crossing $600M ACV with deals in the multi-7-figure range proves enterprise willingness to pay for AI workflow automation. The customer metrics (Lenovo: 35% faster case resolution, Bell: 3M+ deflected support calls) show concrete ROI that justifies the price.

The Counterintuitive Insight

ServiceNow is betting that AI kills SaaS — and it’s a SaaS company. If AI agents can automate the workflows that 100 different point SaaS tools currently handle, the winner is whoever owns the workflow layer, not the feature layer. McDermott’s framing — “functional SaaS will be automated by ServiceNow” — is both a product strategy and a declaration of war on every mid-market SaaS vendor that does one thing well.
