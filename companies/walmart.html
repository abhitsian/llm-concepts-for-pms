Walmart on AI

Deploying AI practically across the world's largest retailer -- from an internal merchant agent ("Wally") to customer-facing search ("Sparky") to supply chain route optimization -- with a "people-led, technology-powered" philosophy.

What the CEO Said

"It's very clear that AI is going to change literally every job. Maybe there's a job in the world that AI won't change, but I haven't thought of it."

Doug McMillon, CEO (WSJ interview, September 2025)

"Wally is learning to help us get to the root cause of issues related to things like out of stocks or overstocks with more accuracy and speed."

Doug McMillon, CEO (Q4 FY2025 earnings call, February 2025)

"These past few quarters, we've talked about how we're using AI. The progress we've made over the years with technology has put us in a position to leverage today's fast-moving capabilities closer to real time."

Doug McMillon, CEO (Q4 FY2025 earnings call, February 2025)

"We've got to create the opportunity for everybody to make it to the other side."

Doug McMillon, CEO, on workforce AI transition (September 2025)

The Numbers

  • FY2025 revenue: $681B; projected FY2026 net sales growth of 3-4%
  • AI-powered route optimization eliminated 30 million unnecessary delivery miles and avoided 94 million lbs (42,000 tons) of CO2 emissions
  • 23 of 42 U.S. regional distribution centers being retrofitted with AI/automation
  • Sam's Club: Exit times 23% faster with AI-powered computer vision cart scanning; 3 in 4 members have friction-free checkout
  • Sam's Club: 40%+ digital engagement via Scan and Go / Just Go AI arches
  • About half of Walmart app users have used Sparky (AI shopping assistant)
  • All Sam's Club field leadership given enterprise ChatGPT access

AI Products & Initiatives

  • Wally -- internal AI agent for merchants, root cause analysis for stock issues
  • Sparky -- customer-facing AI shopping assistant on Walmart app
  • AI-powered route optimization for supply chain and last-mile delivery
  • Computer vision checkout (Sam's Club Just Go / Scan and Go)
  • AI-enhanced product catalog and search
  • Distribution center automation -- goal to upgrade all 42 U.S. regional DCs

The AI Strategy

Walmart is deploying AI with the same operational pragmatism as Costco, but at 10x the scale. Route optimization eliminating 30 million unnecessary delivery miles, pharmacy AI, computer vision checkout at Sam’s Club — these are high-ROI, measurable applications that improve the core business without requiring AI moonshots.

The “people-led, technology-powered” philosophy is a workforce strategy, not just a slogan. With 2.1 million employees, Walmart can’t replace workers with AI — the operational and political consequences would be enormous. Instead, AI augments employees (Wally for merchants, Sparky for customers, route optimization for drivers) while the human workforce remains the execution layer.

The Sam’s Club data (23% faster exit times, 40%+ digital engagement, computer vision checkout) is the most concrete AI retail deployment in this list. Unlike vaporware announcements, these are production systems handling millions of transactions, with measurable improvements in speed and customer satisfaction.

The Counterintuitive Insight

Walmart’s real AI dataset might be more valuable than most tech companies’. Purchase data across every product category, from every zip code in America, updated in real time. While tech companies compete for internet-scraped training data, Walmart has decades of consumer behavior data that reveals what people actually buy — not what they say online. The world’s largest retailer might have the world’s most valuable AI training dataset for commerce, and they haven’t begun to monetize it.
