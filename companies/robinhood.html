Robinhood on AI

Building "Cortex" as a proprietary AI financial assistant to transform both retail and active trading, while using AI to automate 75%+ of customer support.

What the CEO Said

"AI is going to completely transform all aspects of financial services."

Vlad Tenev, CEO, Q4 2025

"Cortex Assistant, which is the AI assistant that's integrated into Robinhood, is rolling out in the coming weeks. So we are refining it internally and that's going to roll out to customers very soon."

Vlad Tenev, CEO, Q4 2025

"I think it's going to become more important to invest, because if you can't rely on labor to generate money to make a living, capital becomes more important. And so we're going to have to make it easier for people to learn about investing."

Vlad Tenev, CEO, 2025

"AI will lead to an explosion of not just new jobs, but new job families."

Vlad Tenev, CEO, 2025

"I've been really proud at how fast we move at Robinhood. Looking ahead, you can really expect us to keep that startup hustle despite our large size."

Vlad Tenev, CEO, Q4 2025

The Numbers

  • Over 75% of customer support cases are now solved by AI, including complex cases that previously required licensed brokerage professionals
  • 2025 revenue of $4.5 billion, up 52% year-over-year
  • Adjusted EBITDA of $2.5 billion, up 76%, with 56% margin
  • Record EPS of $2.05
  • Total Platform Assets grew nearly 70% YoY to $324 billion

AI Products & Initiatives

  • Cortex Assistant (Main App) — AI assistant for retail investors to handle all financial needs: research, portfolio analysis, market insights
  • Cortex for Legend — AI tool for active traders, compared to "Cursor for software engineers," designed to transform the active trading workflow
  • Cortex Digests — AI-powered investment summaries and research digests
  • AI Customer Support — Automated support handling 75%+ of cases including complex brokerage queries

The AI Strategy

Robinhood is running an AI democratization strategy: take the research tools, portfolio analysis, and market insights that institutional investors have and give them to retail investors through Cortex. The “Cursor for software engineers” analogy for Cortex Legend reveals the ambition — an AI that doesn’t just inform but actively assists in trading decisions.

The 75% AI-automated customer support is the near-term efficiency play. But Tenev’s labor thesis is the provocative long-term bet: if AI makes it harder to earn through labor, capital ownership becomes more important. This is a company building an AI product whose addressable market grows as AI eliminates jobs.

The execution speed is the moat. Cortex across both main app and Legend (active trading), plus Cortex Digests — multiple AI products shipping simultaneously. In an accelerated strategic cycle, the company that iterates fastest on AI features wins, because competitive advantages that used to last years now last months.

The Counterintuitive Insight

Robinhood’s CEO makes the most provocative labor argument in this entire list: if AI makes it harder to earn through labor, capital ownership becomes more important. This is a company building an AI product whose addressable market grows as AI eliminates jobs. The thesis is self-reinforcing: more AI disruption = more need for investment platforms = more Robinhood users. The bull case for Robinhood is literally that AI takes everyone’s jobs.
