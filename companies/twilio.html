The Numbers

  • Voice AI revenue growth accelerated above 60% year-over-year in Q4 2025
  • Branded calling revenue grew roughly 6x year-over-year in Q4
  • Full year 2025: $5.1B in revenue (14% YoY growth), $924M non-GAAP operating income, $945M free cash flow
  • Q4 2025: $1.4B revenue, $256M non-GAAP operating income
  • Conversation Relay (AI agent toolkit) seeing rapid adoption in healthcare and other verticals

AI Products & Initiatives

  • Conversation Relay -- framework for building voice AI agents on Twilio infrastructure
  • Branded Calling -- AI-verified caller identity to improve answer rates
  • Conversational Intelligence -- AI-powered call analysis
  • CustomerAI -- AI layer connecting customer data with communication channels
  • Memory-driven orchestration for agentic customer interactions

The AI Strategy

Twilio is the purest “picks and shovels” AI play. Every AI voice agent needs a phone number, SMS capability, and voice processing infrastructure. Twilio sells all of these. Voice AI revenue growing 60%+ YoY and branded calling revenue up 6x shows the thesis working in real numbers.

The transformation from “communications API provider” to “customer experience layer of the internet” reflects the AI opportunity. When enterprises build AI agents that call, text, and answer phones, they all need Twilio’s infrastructure. Conversation Relay (framework for building voice AI agents) positions Twilio as the default platform for voice AI deployment.

“Memory-driven orchestration” is the strategic moat. If Twilio becomes the persistent layer that remembers previous customer interactions across channels, switching away means losing all that context. This is the workflow integration moat: the deeper Twilio embeds into customer communication flows, the harder it is to rip out.

The Counterintuitive Insight

Every AI agent needs a phone number, and Twilio sells phone numbers. As every enterprise builds AI agents that can call, text, and answer phones, they all need Twilio’s infrastructure. This is the purest “picks and shovels” play in the AI era — Twilio doesn’t need to build AI, it needs AI to need phones. Voice AI revenue up 60% YoY proves the plumbing thesis: the most boring layer of the AI stack might be the most durable business.
