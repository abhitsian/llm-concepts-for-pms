BOX

Box on AI

Betting that AI agents will need enterprise data context to be useful, positioning Box as the "content layer" for the agentic enterprise with 100-1000x more AI agent users than human users.

What the CEO Said

"We'll have about 100 times more, maybe 1,000 times more, agents than we have people. So you'll have way more users of that software system, or SaaS, as agents."

Aaron Levie, CEO, 2025

"We are in this window right now that we have not been in for about 15 years, which is — there's a complete platform shift happening in tech that's opening up a spot for a new set of companies to emerge."

Aaron Levie, CEO, 2025

"We are on day one of agent adoption in the enterprise."

Aaron Levie, CEO, 2025

"AI agents are more friend than foe as they make the context and unstructured data on [Box's] platform more valuable."

Aaron Levie, CEO, 2025

The Numbers

  • Enterprise Advanced plans now account for 10% of revenue
  • Upgrades to Enterprise Advanced result in a "rough doubling" of revenue per user vs. non-suite plans
  • Q4 2025 revenue: $306 million, up 9% YoY
  • FY2026 revenue: $1.177 billion, up 8%
  • Net Retention Rate: 102%, up from 101% prior quarter
  • Record gross margin of 81.6% (up 470 bps) and operating margin of 28.4% (up 360 bps)

AI Products & Initiatives

  • Box AI — Native AI capabilities across the Box platform: document intelligence, summarization, Q&A across files
  • Box Enterprise Advanced — Premium tier with AI capabilities that roughly doubles revenue per user; now 10% of total revenue
  • Box AI Agents — Next-generation agentic features including longer-running tasks and complex document processing
  • Box Extract — AI-powered data extraction from documents, planned enhancements for more complex processing
  • Box Automate — Workflow automation platform leveraging AI agents (launched first half of year)
  • AI Unit Pricing Model — Flexible consumption-based pricing where different AI operations cost different amounts of "AI units," moving away from per-seat toward consumption models for agent-driven usage

The AI Strategy

Box exemplifies the AI-Enhanced market where AI makes the existing product more valuable, not obsolete. Enterprise documents are the context AI agents need to be useful — without access to contracts, policies, and procedures, agents can’t do enterprise work. Box is evolving from storage to the “content layer” for AI, a functionality moat where value increases as more agents need enterprise data access.

Levie’s “100-1000x more agents than people” framing reveals the pricing disruption ahead. Per-seat pricing breaks when your most frequent users aren’t human. The AI Unit pricing model — consumption-based, with different operations costing different amounts — is the enterprise pricing model for the agentic era.

The Enterprise Advanced play (10% of revenue, roughly doubling ARPU) shows the monetization path: AI capabilities as an upsell that doubles what customers pay. This is the intelligence gating strategy from AI monetization frameworks — same data, smarter features, higher price.

The Counterintuitive Insight

Box’s pricing shift from per-seat to per-consumption reveals where enterprise AI is really heading. When you have 1,000 AI agents and 10 humans accessing the same content, seat-based pricing is meaningless. Box is one of the first companies to price for a world where machine users outnumber human users 100-to-1 — a pricing model that every SaaS company will eventually need.
