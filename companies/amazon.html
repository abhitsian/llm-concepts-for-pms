The AI Strategy

Amazon is running the classic commoditize-the-complement playbook. Bedrock hosts every competing model (Anthropic, Meta, Mistral, Amazon Titan) — Amazon wins regardless of which model wins. Trainium custom chips commoditize NVIDIA’s GPU advantage with 30-40% better price-performance. The strategy: own the infrastructure layer, let the model layer commoditize above you.

The numbers reveal the scale of the bet: $100B CapEx in 2025, potentially $200B in 2026. AWS AI is growing 3x faster at this stage than AWS itself grew — and AWS became a $142B business. Jassy’s “more demand than supply” framing positions the massive CapEx as demand-driven, not speculative.

The Anthropic partnership ($4B investment, Anthropic running on Trainium) is the clearest signal of Amazon’s strategy: fund the best model maker, run them on your chips, host them on your cloud. Amazon doesn’t need to win the model race. It needs the model race to continue.

The Counterintuitive Insight

Amazon’s AI strategy is to not pick the winning model. By hosting every model on Bedrock and building custom chips that undercut NVIDIA, they’re the arms dealer in a gold rush. The more models compete, the more compute they all need — and AWS provides it. Amazon is the only company in this list that benefits more from AI fragmentation than from AI consolidation.
