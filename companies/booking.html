The Numbers

  • Connected trip transactions grew in the high 20% range annually, now making up a low double-digit percentage of Booking.com total transactions
  • 68 million airline tickets booked across platforms, up 37% YoY
  • Attraction tickets grew nearly 80% YoY (off a small base)
  • AI-powered customer service delivered YoY decrease in customer service costs while growing double-digit on gross bookings and revenue
  • AI integration showing better conversion and lower cancellation rates in early testing
  • 20% rise in travelers booking multiple trip components

AI Products & Initiatives

  • AI Trip Planner — Fully integrated into the Booking.com mobile app in 10+ countries, uses generative AI to build complex itineraries based on conversational prompts
  • Connected Trip Platform — AI-powered cross-vertical booking experience spanning hotels, flights, attractions, and ground transport
  • AI Customer Service — Generative AI integrated into customer service processes, reducing costs while maintaining quality
  • Agentic AI Preparation — Building capabilities to work with AI agent models that will change how customers discover and interact with the platform
  • 2026 Focus — Further connecting agentic capabilities for a more unified and personalized experience

The AI Strategy

Booking is an AI-Enhanced market player with a functionality moat that’s nearly impossible to replicate. Twenty-eight years of travel data, deep supplier integrations across hotels/flights/attractions/ground transport, and billions of completed bookings create a dataset and relationship web that no AI startup can reproduce from scratch.

The “Connected Trip” vision — booking multiple travel components in one transaction — was always the goal but never technically feasible. AI makes it possible by handling the complexity of cross-vertical recommendations and itinerary planning. The 20% rise in multi-component bookings proves the thesis is working.

The agentic AI preparation is the strategic chess move. Booking recognizes that AI agents will eventually book travel on behalf of consumers. But those agents still need supplier integrations, payment processing, and customer service infrastructure. Booking is building the backend that AI agents need to actually complete a booking.

The Counterintuitive Insight

Booking’s biggest AI threat is also its opportunity. Agentic AI could route around travel platforms entirely — but only if it can book hotels, flights, and rental cars in a single transaction with real-time availability. Booking is one of the few companies with the supplier integrations to make that work. AI agents need Booking’s infrastructure more than Booking needs any specific AI model.
