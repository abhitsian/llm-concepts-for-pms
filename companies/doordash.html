DoorDash on AI

Using AI to strengthen its logistics moat -- powering merchant tools, customer service, and voice ordering -- while viewing AI shopping agents as new top-of-funnel channels that still need DoorDash's end-to-end delivery execution.

What the CEO Said

"How well does it solve the end-to-end job for a customer? That becomes the lens in which I approach all of these things."

Tony Xu, CEO, on evaluating AI and autonomous vehicles (Q4 2025, February 2026)

"[AI assistants and agentic commerce are] very much almost like new forms of the Googles or other large kind of top-of-funnel channels."

Tony Xu, CEO (Q4 2025, February 2026)

"This is a topic that is changing literally by the day, maybe by the week. It's been kind of astonishing and almost breathtaking how fast the coding is changing."

Tony Xu, CEO, on AI coding agents (Q4 2025, February 2026)

The Numbers

  • 90%+ daily active usage of coding agents across all DoorDash engineers
  • AI agent launched to help merchants optimize advertising and promotions
  • "Mixed fleet" model in development: Dashers + autonomous vehicles assigned by delivery complexity and distance
  • CFO described logistics efficiency gains as "continual execution, finding basis points" through AI optimization

AI Products & Initiatives

  • AI-powered merchant advertising/promotion agent
  • Voice ordering AI
  • AI customer service automation
  • Coding agent adoption (90%+ daily usage across engineering)
  • Mixed fleet logistics -- human Dashers + autonomous vehicles
  • Agentic commerce positioning -- DoorDash as fulfillment backend for AI shopping agents
