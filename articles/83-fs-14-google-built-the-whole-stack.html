Google Built the Whole Stack

Deep Dive 2 — From Sand to Software
┈┈┈┈

Google is the only company in the world that designs its own AI chips, builds its own data centers, trains its own frontier models, runs its own cloud platform, and distributes AI to 2 billion users through Search, Android, Chrome, YouTube, and Gmail. Nobody else has the complete vertical. Not Microsoft (depends on OpenAI for models, Nvidia for chips). Not Amazon (depends on Anthropic for frontier models, Nvidia for much of its compute). Not Meta (doesn't have a cloud platform or custom chips at TPU scale). Google has all of it.

TPU: Seven Generations Deep

Google announced its first Tensor Processing Unit in 2016 — purpose-built silicon for neural network math. While the rest of the industry was discovering GPUs could do AI, Google was already designing chips specifically for it.

TPU v1 (2016): Inference only. 92 TOPS (tera operations per second). Used internally for Google Search ranking and Google Photos.

TPU v2 (2017): Added training capability. 45 TFLOPS. First available externally through Google Cloud. Connected in "pods" of 256 chips.

TPU v3 (2018): 123 TFLOPS. Liquid-cooled (Google saw the thermal wall coming years before the rest of the industry). Pods of 1,024 chips.

TPU v4 (2021): 275 TFLOPS. 4,096-chip pods with custom optical circuit switching for inter-chip communication. This is the generation that trained PaLM and early Gemini models.

TPU v5e (2023): Cost-optimized for inference. Deliberately traded peak performance for efficiency — reflecting Google's realization that inference economics, not training, would determine the business.

TPU v5p (2023): Training-optimized variant. 459 TFLOPS. Supported the full Gemini training runs.

Trillium / TPU v6 (2024): 4.7x improvement in peak compute per chip over v5e. Custom inter-chip interconnect (ICI) scaling to 256 chips per pod.

Ironwood / TPU v7 (2025): Google's latest. Designed explicitly for the inference-heavy, agentic AI workload pattern. Dylan Patel at SemiAnalysis called it "the most significant custom silicon announcement in AI this year" — not because of raw performance but because of the system-level integration: the chip, the interconnect, the cooling, the software stack all designed as one unit.

The Vertical Integration Advantage

When Google designs a new model architecture for Gemini, the TPU team can co-optimize the hardware. When the TPU team designs a new chip feature, the Gemini team can architect the model to exploit it. This co-design loop is impossible for companies that buy commodity hardware.

Concrete examples of what co-design enables:

SparseCore. TPUs have specialized hardware for sparse computation — operations where most values are zero. Google's recommendation models (Search, YouTube, Ads) are highly sparse. A GPU treats sparse and dense computation the same. TPU's SparseCore accelerates sparse ops by 10x+, which directly translates to lower cost per ad served and per search query.

Inter-chip interconnect (ICI). Google's TPU pods connect chips via a custom optical switching fabric that provides higher bandwidth and lower latency than InfiniBand for the specific communication patterns TPU workloads use. Nvidia's InfiniBand is general-purpose and excellent. Google's ICI is purpose-built and, for Google's workloads, better.

Software stack co-design. Google's XLA compiler generates TPU-specific code that exploits hardware features no general-purpose compiler can target. JAX (Google's ML framework) is designed from the ground up for XLA and TPUs. The result: Google's software extracts a higher percentage of theoretical peak performance from TPUs than most users extract from Nvidia GPUs via CUDA.

The Distribution Weapon

Google's infrastructure advantage is amplified by its distribution. AI Overviews in Search reach 2 billion users in 200+ countries. Gemini is embedded in Android, Chrome, Gmail, Workspace, YouTube, Maps, and Photos. When Google improves its AI — better model, more efficient inference, lower cost per query — it instantly affects billions of interactions.

This creates a flywheel: massive distribution generates massive usage data. Usage data informs model improvement. Better models increase engagement. Engagement justifies infrastructure investment. Investment funds the next TPU generation. The cycle compounds.

Google processes over 1.3 quadrillion tokens per month — up from 480 trillion a year ago. That 20x growth in token volume is the Jevons Paradox in action: cheaper AI (via better TPUs and software) drives more AI usage, which drives more infrastructure investment. Google can afford to operate AI at razor-thin margins or even losses on individual queries because the AI improves its core revenue engine (Search ads, YouTube ads, Cloud).

The Gavin Baker Thesis

Investor Gavin Baker articulated why Google's position is so formidable: Google can "suck the economic oxygen out of the ecosystem" by operating AI at cost or below, funded by its advertising business. A startup charging $20/month for an AI product competes against Google giving comparable capability away for free to 2 billion users. Google doesn't need AI to be profitable on its own. It needs AI to keep Search and YouTube dominant.

This is the low-cost producer thesis applied to AI. When the model commoditizes (and it's commoditizing fast), the winner is whoever can run it cheapest at the largest scale. Google has the cheapest chips (custom TPUs), the largest scale (2B users), and a business model that subsidizes the AI layer (advertising). That's a structural advantage, not a temporary one.

The Vulnerability

Google's vertical integration is also a lock-in. TPUs only run on Google Cloud. If a better model appears that's optimized for Nvidia hardware, Google can't easily switch. The custom software stack (XLA, JAX) means Google's AI engineering talent builds skills that are less portable than CUDA skills. And the co-design advantage requires coordination across chip, software, model, and product teams — a management challenge that grows harder as the organization grows larger.

But for now, Google is the only company that controls the entire pipeline from custom silicon to 2 billion end users. That's a position nobody else can replicate without a decade of investment. And in AI, a decade is several lifetimes.
